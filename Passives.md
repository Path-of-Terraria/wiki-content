# Passive Tree
The **Passive Tree** is the core progression system of the game, allowing players to customize their characters with a vast network of nodes that grant both small statistical bonuses and powerful unique effects. By investing passive points earned through leveling, players shape their builds in highly personalized and thematic ways.

Upon character creation, the player will choose one of 4 classes - Mage, Ranger, Summoner, and Melee. This will define the starting point of their passive tree.

The maximum player level is 100, therefore they will have 100 points to spend.

## Core Concepts
### Clusters and Travel Routes
The tree is made up of travel routes, clusters, keystones, and jewel sockets. A travel route is often strictly attribute bonuses and is meant to be a means of getting to somewhere you want on the tree. Clusters are thematic and often designed in a way that fits the theme. 
Eg. Fire Cluster contains "5% Chance to Ignite on Hit", "5% Increased Fire Damage", and "10% Added Fire Resistance".
### Small Passives
The nodes that make up the majority of the tree. These range from statistical increases to smaller unique effects.
### Masteries
Masteries are obtained when socketing all connected passives in a cluster. Each mastery is thematic to it's cluster and will often contain 2-3+ choices. Each mastery will be significantly more powerful than a regular node, and often have unique effects that alter gameplay significantly.
### Keystones
Keystone passives are powerful, build-defining nodes. Unlike smaller passives or masteries, keystones dramatically change how a character functions by granting a major benefit at the cost of a significant drawback. Not every build will be able to run every keystone, or even a single keystone. 
Eg. "Ignites and Bleeds you inflict deal 200% more Damage BUT you are permanently Ignited, and take 10 fire damage per second."
### Jewel Sockets
Special nodes that allow insertion of jewels, which can either modify the tree itself, or grant passive effects. Jewels can be of the 4 base rarities, Normal, Magic, Rare, and Unique. Unique Jewels will often be massively build altering as you will only ever achieve 1 or 2 jewel sockets in your tree.
