Conflux League

The premise of the Conflux league is to lean into the aspect of the elemental damage of the mob. Furthering out the content for Fire, Cold, Lightning, and the new Chaos damage. More information on these changes in the dedicated section below.

In the Conflux league you will encounter Rifts within your world that contain enemies from beyond your reality. Clear these rifts by killing mobs from the Rift to increase the Stability of your world and close these Rifts. The higher the stability you generate the more rewards you will get.

We don't want to spoil too much and want to let you discover what is out there but the general premise of the Conflux SPECIFIC content is as follows. 

  • 4 New Bosses with their own Domains
  • New “Rift” In-Map Content
  • Dozens of new Uniques
  • Introduction of the Map Device Interface

The Class System

A fundamental change to how Path of Terraria works in 0.3 will be the introduction of the Class System. There are four classes the player can choose from on character creation, and these classes cannot be changed once you have created your character. 
• Mage
• Summoner
• Melee
• Ranged

This core change allows us to do a few things that would otherwise be difficult or confusing to do without classes.
1. Where you start on the new passive tree - Previously there was one starting point, but with the new tree, each class will start in a slightly different cardinal direction of the tree. With travel nodes, you are still able to visit other sides of the tree, but this will slightly encourage the core class archetypes further, and allowing for easier balancing of certain synergies.
2. Future systems regarding any class-specific designs - This includes elements such as sub-class archetypes found in most RPGs, specific questlines tied to each class, and future systems that rely on clear class identity. By establishing distinct class foundations now, we can later introduce meaningful progression paths that deepen each playstyle.
3. Skills - As the skill system has also been mostly overhauled, there are certain elements of skills being involved within the new class system. For example, the mage will start with the fireball skill, and the ranger will start with the rain of arrows skill. If we want a new “tier” of skill to be unlocked at specific quests, like wall of flesh, we can make it so each class will receive their respective skill. 
Important Note: All skills can still be used by every class, what matters primarily is the weapon type. If you want to try out a skill, like rain of arrows, as a mage, this is still doable. Skills will be purchasable via their respective class-specific NPC's. (Alaric being the Mage NPC, for example.)

What This Means

- Each class will have its own identity, starting point, and natural progression path.
- Future updates can now include sub-classes, questlines, etc.
- Builds will feel more cohesive and reflect the strengths of their archetype.
- Balance will be easier to fine-tune around some-what defined archetypes.

What This Doesn’t Mean

- You’re not locked out of using other skills or weapons.
- Hybrid or experimental builds are still viable, just more intentional designs.

We understand that a change of this scale may introduce certain limitations for some existing builds or weapon combinations. However, this shift is a necessary foundation for more meaningful growth in the long term. By defining clearer class boundaries, we can begin to design systems that reward commitment to an archetype, rather than spreading power too thin across all directions. Enhancing existing class fantasy is the goal, as it is an ARPG-inspired mod.

Elemental Damage System Overhaul

In this new league release we wanted to introduce the future of what our elemental damage looks like in the mod. Allowing you to invest in several new angles never before thought possible. Scaling for each of these elemental types or all three should become possible to further enhance your characters. 

  • Changed elemental damage affixes to be exclusive to weapons & occasionally offhands
  • Added elemental damage to the appropriate vanilla items (Fire, Cold, Lightning, Chaos)
  • Reworked all ailments.
    • Ignite - A high damage fire damage DoT that scales with your damage, and is guaranteed on critical strikes. One can be active on a target by default. Any fire damage critical can inflict ignite.
    • Shock - A debuff that applies a damage multiplier to all incoming damage on that enemy. Your hits and shock effectiveness will scale this multiplier. Any lightning damage can inflict shock.
    • Freeze - A debuff that will “freeze” an enemy in place. Duration scales with damage, max enemy hp, and any “freeze duration” modifiers. Any cold damage critical can inflict freeze.
    • Poison - An infinitely stacking DoT that deals steady, ramping chaos damage over time. Chaos damage does NOT inflict poison by default. Specific skills/affixes/weapons can allow poison infliction
    • Bleed - A high damage DoT with limited stacking. Encourages aggressive playstyles to keep stacks up due to quick debuff drop-off rates. This affix can roll on melee & ranged weapons by default.
  • Fixed Shock multiplier not working for projectiles
  • Added "Chance to apply Bleed on hit", "Increased max Bleed stacks", and "More damage used for Bleed" item affixes that can roll on melee or ranged weapons only.
  • Added frozen effect for NPCs, shader and peripherals
  • Fixed a couple minor issues with Cold damage, namely defaulting to a 0.2 conversion multiplier
  • Fixed "healing on killing a burning enemy" affix not applying for Ignited
  • Added conversion and resistance item affixes for Chaos
  • Added Chaotic mob affix
  • Replaced all vanilla Poison effects with a custom stacking Poison, dealing 4 chaos damage per stack
  • Stopped mob affix icons from drawing weirdly when the mob is frozen
  • Changed localizations for any "flat" to be "added" to stay consistent with flat being added bonuses.
  • Reworked tooltips to account for elemental damage. Damage numbers now have color based on their respective element(s).
  • Changes resistance affixes to only be rollable on equipment (non weapons) This ensures weapons are mostly exclusive to damage/utility affixes

 

Enemy Spawning System Overhaul - Encounters

One problem we noticed and wanted to reconcile was that spawning seemed a bit boring, as vanilla spawning is often one or two mobs at the same time. This doesn't really fit with the ARPG experience and thus a new system was implemented that we are calling “Encounters” 

Encounter spawning will be turned on within your explorable and boss domain maps but will not be present in the overworld. Think of this as micro dungeons that just randomly appear throughout your mapping system and trigger when coming within the radius of the encounter spawner. This will enable spawns to come from all directions and contain set amounts of mobs. The count of these mobs, and their strength will be influenced by your map modifiers allowing you to further juice this content.

Virtual Looting Bag

We realize a common annoyance is not having a loot filter. With how terraria works, picking up loot around you instead of a click-to-loot system like other ARPGS have we have come up with a solution to hopefully mitigate this issue.

The virtual loot bag is an optional way of looting. When the virtual loot bag is enabled all GEAR that is looted will go to this virtual bag. The virtual bag is opened when the player goes to leave the explorable map. The virtual loot bag is cleared when an exploration map is left. So the virtual bag clears each map.

Some things to expect

  • New hotkey option to virtual bag at any point
  • New config option to use virtual bag
  • All GEAR picked up when in explorable map goes to virtual bag when enabled
  • Virtual bag is opened when leaving an explorable map automatically
  • Confirm leave button appears to "actually" leave the map once loot is confirmed
  • Can right click gear from main inventory to virtual bag
  • Can right click gear from virtual bag into inventory
  • Virtual bag is (basically) limitless

Beyond this original implementation we will explore being able to further filter loot. Such as an interface for defining your own loot filter system, akin to Last Epoch, where loot will just simply be put into a “Filtered” section of this loot bag so you can re-check your filter to make sure you haven't missed anything important and allow you to further tweak your loot filter settings.
